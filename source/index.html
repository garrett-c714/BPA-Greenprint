Why Bio Friendly?

The Problem At Hand:

Every year 6,870 million metric tons of greenhouse gasses are released into the atmosphere. These greenhouse gasses are fueling the epidemic known as “global warming”. The effects of these emissions can be seen across the United States. California is dealing with carcinogenic smog throughout their urban communities, and raging wildfires in the rural areas. Indiana is addressing a problem concerning excessive trash production. Texas is emitting copious amounts of carbon dioxide into the atmosphere. Wyoming is burning through energy. Kansas, Maryland, and Mississippi are experiencing clean water crises. Louisiana is quickly losing its bayous due to global warming. These are just some examples of how the issue is spread across the country, and not just in concentrated areas. Most of the side effects of global warming can be reduced with simple changes, but to fully eradicate the epidemic, changes need to be made to everyday living. The first place environmentally friendly changes need to be implemented is in households. This is because people spend around 90% of time indoors, and a large portion of this time is spent in one's own house. For the health of the planet, all homes that are currently being built should be required to have some of these bio-friendly qualities.

Environmental Impact

A bio-friendly home is designed with the environment in mind, be it through reducing carbon emissions, saving natural resources, or producing organic food, a bio-friendly home caters to the environment. By harnessing the power of thr sun, wind, and occasionally water to power a home, one can reduce the necessity of coal and natural gas in the process of everyday life, and through this method, begin reducing dangerous carbon emissions. The reduction of carbon emissions is critical to the health of our planet and the survival of our species. Green homes may simply be helpful now, but soon they will become a necessity in keeping our planet alive.

Financial Benefits

Having a bio-friendly home is all but guaranteed to save the consumer money. Despite an initially high start-up cost, the reduced cost of many necessities in one's home is bound to make the home-owner’s start up money back and then some. The various components that are designed to benefit the environment all work together to save money. Solar panels will almost completely negate one's electric bill, while geothermal pumps will save money over time. On top of that, if the consumer opts to install their own greenhouse, and grow their own food, they will save money on their trips to the grocery store. Learn more specifics about savings and start-up costs in our Components page.

Health Benefits

One of the biggest advantages to having an environmentally-friendly home are the health benefits to the owner. Green homes are made out of natural matierals rather than chemically treated ones. Reducing exposure to volatile organic compounds reduces your likely-hood of having health complications in the future. In addition, many eco-homes are designed to bring in more sunlight than traditional home designs. When someone is exposed to sunlight throughout the day, the person's brain releases dopamine. Dopamine is known to make people happier, and prolong their lives. Furthermore, the organic foods grown in a greenhouse will reduce your chemical intake compared to store-bought vegetables. You can learn more about the specific benefits of each aspect of a eco-friendly home in our Components page.
