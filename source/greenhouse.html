How They Work

Greenhouses are simple designs that are universally utilized for all sorts of situations. Greenhouses are buildings constructed mainly out of glass. The glass design allows for light to reach the plants contained within. The glass structure is also more successful at protecting the plants than a traditional fence. The enclosed design keeps animals and other harmful things from reaching the plants. Because of this, the success rate of plant growth is dramatically increased when a greenhouse is introduced. Greenhouses also have a water supply system that waters plants at specified time intervals. Greenhouses are not just beneficial at increasing plant growth rates, but they also reduce the amount of trips to the grocery store one must make. This means that less harmful emissions are reduced into the atmosphere. Greenhouses are just one way someone can reduce their carbon footprint.

Benefits And Savings?

The main benefits that come along with owning a greenhouse are financial and health benefits. Vegetables and other produce produced within the greenhouse are free from toxins in the environment such as pesticides and acid rain. This makes the produce much healthier than produce found within a grocery store. Along with the health benefits, owning a greenhouse reduces one's trips to a grocery store. By cutting down the amount of times one is on the road, the amount of harmful emmisions released into the atmosphere is also reduced.

Potential Challenges

There aren't many HOAs with regulations against greenhouses. As long as an HOA allows exterior buildings, greenhouses should be allowed, but one should consult their HOA before building.

There are many insurance options when it comes to covering a greenhouse, and there should be no problem in attaining one.

If the greenhouse is attached to the house, a permit is required because it an addition to the house. If not, a permit is not required.
