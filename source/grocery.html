How They Work

Many grocery stores now offer a service that allows customers to order their groceries online through their website, rather than having to go to the store yourself. The grocery store will then send out a truck loaded up with several customers groceries. This may not seem better than each person going to the grocery store, but it is essentially the same as car pooling. Rather than each customer driving to the store and back, they allow the groceries to be done in one round trip. One round trip is less driving than each car going back and forth, and therefore produces less emissions.

Benefits And Savings?

Ordering grocieries for delivery online doesn't save the consumer any money, however, it saves the consumer time. In addition, the way grocery delivery works allows less overall driving between grocery store and home, and thus reduces greenhouse emissions by a small margin.

Potential Challenges

Some HOAs don't like when large trucks are constantly entering and exiting a neighborhood, but buying in bulk and less often should solve this issue.
