How They Work

Solar panels are made up of a system of small units known as “solar cells”. These cells are composed of two layers of crystal and silicon. The solar cells capture photons contained in sunlight. The photons bounce back and forth between the two layers causing silicon electrons to dislodge. These electrons then cycle through a house, and are able to be used as electricity. Once the energy from the electrons has been used, the electron makes its way back in the silicon layer. Because of this continuous cycle, solar cells can last for decades. The longevity of the system and abundance of solar energy make many people believe the world will run on solar power in the future.

Energy Efficiency

Tax Credit Deduction

On average, solar panels can convert 15%-20% of the sun's energy into usable and clean electricity, compared to fossil fuels, that harness only 40% of the potential energy, wasting nearly 60% while harming the environment in the process. They payback the initial cost in only 4-5 years on average. Solar panels are at peak performance for the first 12 years, but they tend to live as long as 20-25 years. The government will payback 26% of the initial cost through a tax credit. Solar panels fall just behind wind turbines for the cheapest energy per kilowatt hour.

Potential Challenges

The solar panel systems as a whole is currently not recyclable. This can make them an environmental concern once their lifetimes have ran their course, but pieces of the system such as the silicon solar cells, metal framing, glass sheets, wires, and plexiglass.

Use-By-Right allows a small set of solar panels to be installed with minimal zoning regulations. With additional pemits an entire solar panel system can be installed.

The Home Owners Associations cannot legally regulate the installation of a solar panels system unless major quantities are installed. Most HOAs don't have any regulations regarding solar panels.

Micro cracks can occur in solar panels when they are not maintained, but with the correct maintenance solar panels will live out their entire lives.

Most home-owner policies cover solar panels; however, one should contact their insurance provider before installing.

One must acquire a building permit before installation. Most permits are fairly easy to obtain.

Solar panels rely on the sun's light energy to create energy. They are most effective in areas with optimal sunlight, but they are useful in almost all climates.
