How They Work

When rainwater lands on the roof of a home, gravity pulls the water downward off the roof where it runs into the pipes of the rainwater collection system. The pipes then all lead to one of 3 200-gallon drums. These dums store the water until its is needed. The water can be used in places such as a greenhouse, or it can be run through a series of filters and used somewhere within the household.

Benefits And Savings?

The main benefit of the rainwater collection system is the reduction in water bills. The system holds enough water that in areas with moderate rainfall, a household should be able to be solely provided by the system. In areas with less rain, the system can help provide water occasionally, which will still reduce one's water bill even if it is just slightly.

Potential Challenges

There aren't many HOAs with regulations against rainwater collection systems, but one should consult their HOA before building.

There aren't a lot of insurance options when it comes to irrigation systems, but there are a few that will cover the structure.

Dry spells can be hard on a rainwater collection system, but due to its massive holding tanks, the system can hold water for weeks without rain.
