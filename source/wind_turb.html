How They Work

Wind turbines harness the wind’s energy and transform it into electricity. They do this through a series of reactions. The turbine’s propellers are designed to spin at the slightest amount of wind. The energy produced from the rotation of the blades is then transferred to a generator where it can be turned into electricity. When wind gusts are less than 12 mph, the rotation of the blades is not strong enough to allow the generator to produce electricity. Engineers were able to address this problem by adding a system of gears between the propellers and the generator. The added gears are significantly smaller than the propellers, so the spin rate of these gears is 90 times higher than that of the propellers. The kinetic energy created by these gears is then captured by the generator and converted into electricity. This gear system is able to detect high wind situations and disconnect certain gears to cut off the speed at a maximum of 80 kilometers per hour. This prevents damage to the system caused by excessive speeds. The electricity produced in the generator is then transferred through a series of cables to the base of the turbine and fed into a transformer, where it is then able to be dispersed to its desired destinations.

Energy Efficiency

Tax Credit Condition

Wind turbine’s cost per KwH is about $0.04, compared to solar panel’s cost per KwH of $0.10, and standard fossils fuels cost per KwH of $0.11. On average, wind turbines can convert 60%-70% of the wind’s energy into usable and clean electricity, compared to fossil fuels, that harness only 40% of the potential energy, wasting nearly 60% while harming the environment in the process. The government offers a tax reduction of 30% percent of wind turbines and their installation, which can be taken off of your federal taxes.

Potential Challenges

Zoning is a way for the local government to be able to regulate and have control of how the land owner develops the land. In residential zones, structures typically up to ~35 ft are a “permitted use” only.

All associations are different, but the majority allow residential wind turbines; however, they may have their own requirements, therefore be sure to contact them before installation.

Some utilities require owners to maintain liability insurance to protect them from liability for facilities they do not own.

Permits control how the turbines are installed. One can contact local authorities to learn what type of permitting is needed. They often add substantial costs to the project.
