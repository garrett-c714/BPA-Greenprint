How They Work

Electric cars work the same as a regular car with one key difference. Instead of primarily running on gas, they run on electricity. Electric cars are charged, typically overnight, while they are not in use. This reduces the money spent on gas, and reduces carbon dioxide emissions. Electric cars are ideal for daily commuting because everyday one will have a full tank of "gas". That being said, one downside to electric cars is that for very long distance trips one will have to charge during the trip if the tank is running low. In addition the cars can only charge in areas that have the specific charging stations.

Energy Efficiency

Electric cars usually pay back for themselves over a 20-25 year span, but they last for over 25 years on average. They have a 67% energy efficiency, which isn't comparable to normal gas guzzling vehicles because they don't use electricity.

Potential Challenges

Electric cars require a charger. The type of cord will vary based on the car model. You can view the types of chargers here. Be sure to have one in the car at all times, and it may be a good idea to have one to keep at home as well as a spare.

Insurance can be expensive, but it does exist and as with normal auto insurance is required. In addition, Tesla offers its own insurance for a lower rate for customers who own Tesla cars.

Electric cars require charging about once a week. Before a long trip be sure that the battery is charged, and plan in advance to stop at charging stations.
